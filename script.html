Notes for the reviewer. Thank you for checking this. A few things are deliberate, so please suggest changes within them:
  • The shape: slides 1-5 are what we do, slides 6-8 are three real customers (what problem they had, what we built, how they use it today, what they gained), slide 9 is the give, slide 10 is the ask.
  • Very simple English is intentional. Short sentences, everyday words. The audience listens once; nobody can re-read.
  • Hard limit of 5 minutes. If you add words somewhere, please suggest a cut somewhere else. Lines marked in yellow are the first ones to cut if we run long.
  • Some words are off-limits because other members of the chapter own those businesses: leads / lead generation, digital marketing, website, LinkedIn, social media, SEO, ads, campaigns, CRM, branding. We say: enquiries, customers, quotations, AI staff, automation, audit.
  • Slide 9's company names are a draft - Shruti to confirm which introductions to offer publicly.
  • The best way to review: open the presentation link above, click through it once, then read this script alongside.
1Jarvis Daily~32 sec
On screen
Everyone talks about AI. Nobody knows where it fits.
Shruti Pethkar. Jarvis Daily. We build AI employees for businesses.

I am Shruti Pethkar, from Jarvis Daily. We build AI employees for businesses. When I tell people this, I get three reactions. Some think robots will take over their office. Some think it is all a scam. And one uncle asked me if AI can find a wife for his son. Everyone is talking about AI. Almost nobody knows where it fits in their own business. Pause for the smile after the uncle line. Then slow down for the last two sentences.

The same answers on WhatsApp. The same quotation, again and again. The same follow-up. Every business has this work. It quietly eats your team's whole day.
